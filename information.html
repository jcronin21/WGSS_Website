Introduction

In the realm of computing, there exist explicit and implicit challenges concerning race and ethnicity. Scholars like Ihudiya Finda Ogbonnaya-Ogburu have shed light on these issues, specifically within organizations like SIGCHI. Ogbonnaya-Ogburu's insights emphasize the importance of addressing these challenges, as they affect not only the field of computing but also broader societal systems.

Insights from:Anti-Racism in Action: A Speculative Design Approach to Reimagining SIGCHI

Connecting to Literature and Womanhood

One notable segment by Ihudiya Finda Ogbonnaya-Ogburu raises crucial points about the representation and inclusion of racial and ethnic minorities within computing communities. The lack of diversity in images, research groups, and events serves as a demonstration of the broken systems within these spaces. Quote: "As an organization, we have yet to collectively deep dive into establishing policies and actionable approaches to address issues related to race and ethnicity. My presence as the sole African American woman in many spaces should serve as a reminder that our systems are broken." Connecting to Literature and Womanhood This discussion resonates deeply with our exploration of womanhood in African American literature. The same struggles for representation and acknowledgment of diversity within computing intersect with the portrayal and recognition of African American womanhood in literature and academic studies.
